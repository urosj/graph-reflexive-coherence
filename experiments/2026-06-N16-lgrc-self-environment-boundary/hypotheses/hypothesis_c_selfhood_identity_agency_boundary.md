# Hypothesis C - Selfhood, Identity, And Agency Boundary

## Claim

Even if internal/external separability is observed, selfhood, personhood,
identity acceptance, semantic goal ownership, intention, agency, native support,
fully native integration, and unrestricted agency remain blocked unless
separate theory and native implementation gates are satisfied.

Selective uptake, resource assimilation, porous boundary function,
agency-like boundary behavior, semantic recognition, organism claims, and life
claims also remain out of N16 scope.

## Required Evidence

- Claim flags forced false for unsafe promotions.
- Boundary rows for selfhood, personhood, identity acceptance, semantic goal
  ownership, intention, agency, native support, fully native integration, and
  unrestricted agency.
- Dedicated relabel controls where available.
- Explicit statement that Phase 8 remains unopened unless separately requested.

## Controls

- Identity acceptance relabel blocked.
- Selfhood/personhood relabel blocked.
- Semantic goal ownership relabel blocked.
- Native support relabel blocked.
- Agency and unrestricted agency relabels remain unsupported.

## Non-Claims

This hypothesis is a claim-boundary hypothesis. It does not itself provide AP6
positive evidence; it prevents AP6 evidence from being promoted into stronger
unsupported claims.
