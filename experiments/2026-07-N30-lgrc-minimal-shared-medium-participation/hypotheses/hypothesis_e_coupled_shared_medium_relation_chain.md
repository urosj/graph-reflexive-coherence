# Hypothesis E - Coupled Shared-Medium Relation Chain

## Claim

N30 can show that participant attribution, medium perturbation, trace/surface
change, and later eligibility change belong to one source-current relation
chain.

## Expected Support

Hypothesis E is supported if candidate rows record:

- participant carrier and bounded persistence window;
- perturbation event on a declared non-private medium surface;
- trace or surface-state change with persistence/decay evidence;
- later eligibility, cost, support, routing, susceptibility, or capacity
  change;
- causal ordering between participant event, perturbation, trace, and later
  response;
- controls showing the later response depends on the declared medium surface.

## Failure Conditions

Hypothesis E fails if participant continuity, trace formation, and later
eligibility change are only separately present, post-hoc linked, label-linked,
or carried by direct message, hidden producer, global controller, or private
participant state.
