# Hypothesis C - Medium Surface, Trace, And Eligibility Evidence

## Claim

N30 can produce source-current evidence that a declared medium surface is
perturbed, leaves a trace or surface change, and later alters eligibility,
cost, support, routing, susceptibility, or capacity for a participant.

## Expected Support

Hypothesis C is supported if candidate rows record:

- medium surface identity and carrier field;
- perturbation source and timing;
- trace / decay / persistence evidence;
- later susceptibility or eligibility change;
- N28-style distinction between generative, extractive, competitive, neutral,
  or non-effect cases where relevant;
- replay and controls showing the effect is not direct message passing,
  generic redistribution, hidden producer routing, label-only trace, or global
  scheduler drift.

## Failure Conditions

Hypothesis C fails if the medium is passive or unnamed, no source-current
surface changes, later eligibility is not conditioned by the changed surface,
or the effect depends on direct sender-receiver scripting without recording
medium debt.
