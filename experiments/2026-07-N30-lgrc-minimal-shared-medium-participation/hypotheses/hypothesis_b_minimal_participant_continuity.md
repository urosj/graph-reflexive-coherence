# Hypothesis B - Minimal Participant Continuity And Attribution

## Claim

N30 can define and test a minimally stable participant without claiming
selfhood, semantic identity, agency, or native support.

## Expected Support

Hypothesis B is supported if candidate rows record:

- an identifiable participant carrier;
- a bounded persistence window;
- source-current interaction with a declared medium surface;
- distinguishable contribution or later response;
- replay / transfer discipline informed by N27;
- blocked selfhood, identity, agency, and semantic participant relabels.

## Failure Conditions

Hypothesis B fails if participant continuity is label-only, post-hoc,
producer-carried without debt, not replayable, not attributable to a stable
carrier, or overpromoted into selfhood, identity acceptance, or agency.
