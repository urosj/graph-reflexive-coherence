# Hypothesis B - Observable Versus Causal Distinction

## Statement

N31 can distinguish a fading derived graph observable from a causal
continuation relation and from instantaneous state/flux geometry.

## Required Support

- D0b finite-window relation is exactly defined and recomputable from admitted
  source-current history.
- Cache removal and recomputation produce the same value.
- Cached diagnostic state has no independent causal freedom.
- D0c instantaneous geometry is measured separately after current withdrawal.
- Every organization observable declares whether it is native state, an exact
  or lossy projection, a recomputable history functional, an effective closure,
  independent state, or missing.
- A disconnected-observable control tests whether the reported relation itself
  affects later transport.
- Curvature, alignment, and arrival-distribution observables are each kept below
  causal status until a local mediator intervention changes later readout.
- Causal language is blocked when only the observable fades.

## Failure Conditions

- A fading report value is called a causal trail without mediator evidence.
- Cached history is fed back into transport while still called coherence-only.
- The cache cannot be exactly reconstructed from declared history.
- Instantaneous anisotropy is relabeled as durable aftereffect.
- A mass decrease or diagnostic change is used as causal-mediation evidence
  without a separately validated organization intervention.
- A widened arrival histogram is promoted to temporal D0a, or a flatter
  geometry to weaker guidance, without native local-readout dependence.
- Continued propagation of forming packets is called a later independent route
  effect, or a diagnostic domain is labeled as the causal mediator.

## Claim Ceiling

```text
bounded fading derived graph observable below causal trail
```

or, for D0c alone:

```text
instantaneous state/flux-derived geometry comparator
```
