# Hypothesis D - Conservation, Internal Time, And Local Encounter

## Statement

Any admitted N31 weakening relation can preserve the relevant invariant and use
a declared system-internal clock. Any relation admitted at DR4 or above must
affect later response through a local encounter rather than hidden
destruction or global route selection.

## Required Support

- Closed-system node-plus-in-flight coherence remains invariant within a
  predeclared tolerance.
- Any non-coherence state has declared units, bounds, cost, and invariant.
- Event/proper-time ownership and the advancing event are explicit.
- Wall-clock, process scheduling, and run duration are excluded.
- For DR4+ rows, later readout consumes route-local state or transport.
- Destination/accounting audits exist for moved coherence.
- Route-local mass change closes against signed net boundary flux under the
  frozen support, measure, in-flight treatment, and tolerance.
- In-flight packet amount remains fixed from departure through arrival.
- A declared receiver is outside the later read path during an isolated B-R
  probe unless receiver feedback is itself the registered relation under test.

## Failure Conditions

- Coherence disappears under a multiplicative decay coefficient.
- An in-flight packet arrives with less amount than was debited.
- Local depletion is called decay with no destination.
- Lower route mass is called conservative export without closed route-boundary accounting.
- A global route comparator or semantic label writes the expected result.
- A hidden producer clock advances the lifecycle.

## Claim Effect

Hypotheses A, B, or C cannot support DR4+ when Hypothesis D fails.
