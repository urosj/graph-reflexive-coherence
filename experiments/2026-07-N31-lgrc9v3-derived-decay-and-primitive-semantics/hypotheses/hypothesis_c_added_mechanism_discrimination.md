# Hypothesis C - Added-Mechanism Semantic Discrimination

## Statement

If coherence-only D0 is insufficient, N31 can compare release-efficacy
attenuation, coherence-conserving leakage, and route-susceptibility relaxation
without forcing them into one topology, equation, invariant, or claim.

## Required Support

- Each candidate declares its carrier, units, internal clock, update phase,
  invariant, topology, local encounter, producer role, and restoration state.
- A never attenuates an already-created in-flight packet.
- B records exact source debit, in-flight amount, destination credit, and total
  conservation.
- B-R records the added export-policy owner and inputs, bounds emitted amount by
  available source excess, and remains distinct from ordinary D0-R export.
- B-R may be called a bounded D0-R analogue only when a separate bridge result
  supports the registered carrier trajectory and local readout relation.
- C records independent-state or closure status and does not masquerade as
  coherence-only memory.
- Candidate-specific controls fail closed.
- Raw effect magnitude is not used to rank nonequivalent mechanisms.
- Multiple mechanisms or non-selection remain allowed outcomes.

## Failure Conditions

- All candidates are represented by one generic scalar decay factor.
- Local loss has no destination.
- Wall time drives attenuation or relaxation.
- Route labels or global scores select the later response.
- Producer-mediated success is promoted to native capability.
- Conservation alone is used to promote B-R into ordinary D0-R.

## Claim Ceiling

```text
bounded added-mechanism primitive, closure, extension, or non-selection result
```
