# N31 - LGRC9V3 Derived Decay And Primitive Semantics

N31 is the first graph-side experiment selected by the post-N30
graph/ecology spiral. It responds to a concrete demand from Agentic Ecology:
before a trail-like or stigmergic field can use the word `decay`, the graph
substrate must determine what weakens, what carries the weakening, how internal
time advances it, where conserved coherence goes, and whether the relation is
derived or independently causal.

N31 does not begin by adding a generic decay coefficient. It begins with the
lower question:

```text
Can ordinary C/J_C evolution produce a causal, route-local,
durable-yet-weakening relation without any additional decay state?
```

If not, N31 must distinguish the missing representation or causal surface from
three materially different added-mechanism candidates. Selection is not
mandatory.

## Why N31 Exists

N30 established bounded minimal shared-medium participation: a participant can
change a declared non-private medium surface and a later eligibility relation
can depend on that changed surface. N30 deliberately did not define a general
trace lifecycle or select N31.

The Agentic Ecology P2-I3 trail/stigmergic-field lane then asked for a
non-static trace. Its Q-005 study found that `decay` was hiding several
different operations:

```text
release-efficacy attenuation
coherence-conserving source leakage
route-susceptibility relaxation
```

A subsequent coherence-only review identified a prior possibility:

```text
ordinary coherence redistribution and induced geometry may already produce
the required weakening, with no additional decay state.
```

RCAE therefore paused instead of choosing a substrate mechanism inside the
ecology project. N31 is the downward-discovery part of the intended spiral:

```text
N30 shared-medium minimum
  -> RCAE ecology demand
  -> N31 graph-side semantic and runtime discrimination
  -> exact N31 return bundle
  -> RCAE re-admission and composition
```

The RCAE demand is an input question, not graph evidence.

## Core Question

```text
Can ordinary C/J_C evolution produce a causal, durable-yet-weakening
route-local relation under system-internal time while preserving coherence,
causal locality, restoration identity, and composability without adding a
decay state?

If not, what additional mechanism, effective closure, runtime extension, or
theory extension is actually required, and what is its honest authority?
```

## Candidate Semantics

N31 preserves six distinct candidate meanings.

The D0/B export distinction is a refinement inside this taxonomy, not a
seventh candidate:

```text
D0:
  ordinary admitted evolution weakens the route relation

D0-R:
  ordinary admitted evolution weakens it through conservative net export
  from a registered route region

B-R:
  an added policy determines whether, when, how much, or where to export
```

Conservation does not turn B-R into D0-R. B-R remains a Candidate B result
unless a separate bounded bridge demonstrates that its carrier trajectory and
local readout preserve the registered D0-R relation.

### D0a - Slow Causal Coherence Organization

```text
route activity changes complete C/J_C organization
-> the changed coherence state induces route-local geometry or response
-> forming activity stops
-> ordinary internal coherence evolution weakens the organization
-> later local transport changes because it encounters that organization
```

D0a permits no independently evolving route-memory, age, susceptibility, or
decay state. Complete admitted LGRC causal state still includes packet queues,
in-flight coherence, event ordering, proper time, and frozen constitutive
policies.

### D0b - Fading Derived Graph Observable

```text
recent route flux enters a finite-window derived graph relation
-> forming activity stops
-> old flux leaves the window
-> the reported relation weakens
```

D0b is theory-grounded as a derived observable. It is below a causal trail
unless the underlying coherence organization, rather than a diagnostic label,
is shown to mediate later local transport.

### D0c - Instantaneous State/Flux Geometry

```text
g(t) = geometry(C(t), gradient(C(t)), J_C(t))
```

D0c is a lower comparator. If the route effect disappears immediately when
current stops, it supports current-indexed anisotropy but not a
durable-yet-weakening aftereffect.

### Route Mass, Organization, And Causal Mediation

N31 treats these as three separate claims:

```text
route mass:
  coherence measured over a registered route support and boundary

route organization:
  the admitted C/J_C-derived geometry, coupling, or other route-local relation

causal mediation:
  dependence of a later local readout on that route organization
```

A lower route mass does not by itself prove that route organization weakened,
and either change may occur without mediating later transport. Positive rows
must therefore carry separate mass, organization, and mediation contracts.
The resulting distinctions include:

```text
mass decreases but organization does not weaken:
  conservative redistribution only

organization weakens at matched mass and mediates later readout:
  non-export D0 candidate

mass and organization weaken but mediation is unresolved:
  correlated weakening only

mass and organization weaken, mediation passes, no added export policy:
  D0-R candidate

mass and organization weaken, mediation passes, added export policy present:
  B-R candidate
```

### A - Release-Efficacy Attenuation

The carrier remains, but registered internal age or phase changes the amount
of a newly created response packet. Existing in-flight packet amount may not
attenuate. This may classify as expression attenuation rather than field-state
decay.

### B - Coherence-Conserving Source Leakage

Local source coherence is emitted through declared packet transfer to a
declared destination. Local magnitude decreases while total node-plus-in-flight
coherence remains invariant. B is redistribution, not destruction.

Ordinary outward flux remains D0 when it follows existing C/J_C dynamics. It
becomes B only when a new route-field-specific emission eligibility, amount,
timing, destination, or lifecycle policy is introduced.

### C - Route-Susceptibility Relaxation

A route-local conductance or susceptibility changes after use and later
relaxes under an explicit internal-time law. If this state changes the future
while complete coherence state is otherwise equal, it is an effective closure
or additional causal state, not coherence-only D0.

## Non-Negotiable Boundaries

### Coherence Conservation

For a closed registered LGRC9V3 system:

```text
sum(node coherence) + sum(in-flight packet coherence) = constant
```

An in-flight packet cannot lose amount merely because time elapsed. Local loss
must have a declared destination. A non-coherence state requires its own
invariant, units, cost, and restoration semantics.

### System-Internal Time

Any weakening must advance through event order, event-time keys, node proper
time, or another registered system-internal clock. Wall time, process
scheduling, and test duration are forbidden causal inputs.

### Local Encounter

Later response must encounter the changed route-local state through admitted
local transport. Reading both routes, choosing the larger score, and writing a
preferred result is a global selector, not a causal local encounter.

### Independent Mass, Organization, And Mediation Evidence

Every positive causal-decay row must independently define and measure route
mass, route organization, and causal mediation. The route-mass contract must
close its signed boundary-flux continuity relation. The organization contract
must declare whether its observable is native state, an exact or lossy
projection, a recomputable history functional, an effective closure,
independent state, or missing. A strong organization-mediation intervention
holds route mass and all other continuation state equal; otherwise the result
is bounded partial mediation rather than full organization mediation.

### Representation Before Evidence

D0a must receive one pre-execution representation classification:

```text
represented_natively
represented_by_exact_projection
represented_only_by_lossy_coarse_state
missing
```

A lossy or missing representation blocks positive D0a evidence. N31 must not
invent a persistent `C_slow` scalar merely to complete the controls.

### Identity And Restoration

Reset-sensitive N31 rows must consume:

```text
lgrc9v3_restoration_identity_v2
```

V1 remains a current-state identity and cannot establish equal reset
semantics. External experiment state, if any, must be composed and restored
separately.

## Local Ladders

### Decay-Relation Ladder

```text
DR0 = no source-current decay-relation evidence
```

DR3 if it does not mediate later transport.

primitive succeed.

## Runtime Modification Boundary

N31 is an evidence experiment. Its experiment branch may add scripts,
fixtures, outputs, reports, and requirements records, but it does not modify
`src/` to make a candidate pass.

If N31 selects a missing native mechanism:

```text
N31 classification / requirements
  -> separate graph implementation tranche
  -> source-current validation against the frozen N31 contract
  -> N31 closeout and RCAE return
```

The final N31 return may cite that separate implementation revision. It may not
silently treat a producer candidate as native.
